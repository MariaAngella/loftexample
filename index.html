Bwindi Impenetrable National Park

This biologically diverse region provides shelter to 120 mammals other than mountain gorillas, which include several primate species such as baboons and chimpanzees, elephants and antelopes. There is an estimated 350 bird species in this forest, plus 23 Albertine Rift endemics.

Kidepo Valley National Park

You will have a change to see Zebras, Giraffes, Lions, Cheetahs, the uncommon White Buffalos and several other wildlife such as elephants, leopards, bush duikers, jackals, bush bucks, bush pigs, and a lot more which are sometimes seen from the verandas of your accommodation within the Park.

Learn more
Mount Elgon National Park

A climb on Mt. Elgon’s deserted moorlands unveils a magnificent and uncluttered wilderness without the summit-oriented approach common to many mountains: the main goal of reaching the top of Mt. Elgon is not the final ascent to the 4321m Wagagai Peak, but the descent into the vast 40km caldera.

Learn more
Murchison Falls National Park

Murchison Falls National Park sits on the shore of Lake Albert, in northwest Uganda. It’s known for Murchison Falls, where the Victoria Nile River surges through a narrow gap over a massive drop. Park wildlife includes elephants and hippos, and there are chimpanzees in the Kaniyo Pabidi mahogany forest. The Lake Albert Delta is home to rare shoebill storks. There are game fish in the cascades of Karuma Falls.

Learn more
Queen Elizabeth National Park

Queen Elizabeth National Park is known for its wildlife, including African buffalo, Ugandan kob, hippopotamus, giant forest hog, warthog, Nile crocodile, African bush elephant, African leopard, lion, and chimpanzee. It is home to 95 mammal species and over 500 bird species.
